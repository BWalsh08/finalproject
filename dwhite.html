Derrick White

On February 10, 2022, White was traded to the Boston Celtics in exchange for Josh Richardson, Romeo Langford, a 2022 first-round pick (which turned into Blake Wesley) and the rights to swap 2028 first-round picks. White played his first game with the Celtics a day later and finished with 15 points, 6 rebounds and 2 assists.

White began the 2022-23 season as a starter for the Celtics. On February 10, he scored a then-career-high 33 points against the Charlotte Hornets in a 127 to 116 victory. Three days later on February 13, 2023, White was named Eastern Conference Player of the Week, after averaging 24.5 points, 4.8 rebounds, and 7.5 assists as a starter in place of the injured Marcus Smart.

On March 18, 2024, White put up his first career triple-double with 22 points, 10 rebounds, and 10 assists in a 119 to 94 victory over the Detroit Pistons. On April 29, White registered a career-high 38 points in a 102 to 88 road victory against the Heat in Game 4 of the first round of the playoffs.
