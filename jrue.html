Jrue Holiday

On May 24, 2021, Holiday recorded a postseason career-high 15 assists, alongside 11 points and 7 rebounds, in a 132 to 98 Game 2 win against the Miami Heat in their first round playoff series. The Bucks eventually won the series 4 to 0. In Game 6 of the Conference Finals against the Atlanta Hawks, Holiday dropped 27 points, nine rebounds, and nine assists in a 118 to 107 win, leading the Bucks to the NBA Finals for the first time since 1974. In Game 5 of the NBA Finals against the Phoenix Suns, Holiday put up 27 points, 4 rebounds, 13 assists, and recorded a critical steal from Devin Booker, which led to an alley oop to Giannis Antetokounmpo with less than 20 seconds left in the fourth quarter to help the Bucks win 123 to 119. In Game 6, Holiday recorded a near triple-double with 12 points, 9 rebounds and 11 assists, to help Milwaukee to a 10 to 98 victory, closing out the Suns 4 to 2, securing the 2021 NBA title for the Bucks and awarding Holiday his first championship.

On February 2, Holiday was named to the 2023 NBA All-Star Game as a reserve guard for the Eastern Conference for the first time in 10 seasons, the longest gap between selections for any player in NBA history. On February 14, Holiday tied a then career-high 40 points, with a career-high eight three-point shots made, during a 131 to 125 overtime win against the Boston Celtics. On March 29, Holiday scored a career-high 51 points, along with 8 rebounds and 8 assists on 67% field goal percentage in 32 minutes, leading the Bucks to a 149 to 136 victory over the Indiana Pacers.[69] On April 5, Holiday recorded 20 points, 8 rebounds and a season-high 15 assists in a 105 to 92 win over the Chicago Bulls, wrapping up the top seed in the NBA playoffs for the Milwaukee Bucks. On April 16, in the opening game of the playoffs Holiday posted 16 points, seven rebounds and a playoff career-high 16 assists in a 130 to 117 loss against the Miami Heat. In Game 2 of the Bucks' first round playoff series, Holiday had 24 points, 5 rebounds and 11 assists in a 138 to 122 win, tying the series at a game apiece. In game 5, the Bucks were eliminated after their third straight loss to the Heat.

On September 27, 2023, Holiday, alongside Deandre Ayton, Toumani Camara and a 2029 first-round draft pick was traded to the Portland Trail Blazers as part of a three-team deal that sent Damian Lillard to the Milwaukee Bucks and Grayson Allen, Jusuf NurkiÄ‡, Nassir Little, and Keon Johnson to the Phoenix Suns. Four days later, he was traded to the Boston Celtics in exchange for Robert Williams III, Malcolm Brogdon, and two future first-round draft picks. On April 10, 2024, Holiday agreed to a 4-year, $135 million contract extension with the Celtics with $100 million guaranteed, which would run until the end of the 2027/2028 season.
